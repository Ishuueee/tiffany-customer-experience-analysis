# Tiffany Customer Experience Analysis

## Overview

This project is a real-world customer experience study of Tiffany & Co. Champs-Élysées store in Paris. The goal was to understand how the brand delivers its luxury experience across both physical and digital touchpoints.

What makes this project special is that it is not just theoretical — I personally visited the store with my group members as part of a mystery shopping exercise to experience the customer journey first-hand.

## What We Did

As part of this study, my group and I visited the Tiffany & Co. store in Paris and observed the full customer journey from entry to purchase interaction. We experienced the store as different types of customers:

* First-time visitors
* Gift buyers
* Tourists exploring luxury retail

We focused on how customers are welcomed, guided, and engaged throughout the store experience.

## Objective

The main aim of this project was to compare:

* The **online brand image and storytelling**
* vs. the **actual in-store customer experience**

We wanted to understand whether the emotional luxury promised online is truly delivered in real life.

## Key Observations

During our visit, we noticed:

* The store has a very elegant and premium visual identity
* Staff were polite and professional, but interactions felt more transactional than emotional
* The experience was smooth but lacked deeper storytelling or personalization
* Digital branding felt more immersive compared to the physical store experience

## Methodology

We used a mystery shopping approach combined with:

* Customer journey mapping
* Omnichannel experience analysis
* In-store behavioral observation
* Digital and social media review

## Insights

* Strong luxury positioning and brand image
* Clear and structured customer flow in-store
* Gap between digital storytelling and physical experience
* Opportunity to create more emotional and personalized interactions

## Recommendations

* Introduce storytelling-based selling instead of transactional conversations
* Add more immersive in-store experiences (music, scent, rituals)
* Improve personalization through tools like a “Love Profile” concept
* Strengthen alignment between digital campaigns and store experience

## What I Learned

This project helped me understand how luxury retail is not just about products, but about emotions, storytelling, and experience design. Visiting the store in person made the analysis much more real and insightful.
